Folklore & Forgotten Myths

This archive contains the ancient whispers and cultural ghosts that inspired my saga.
Some are real. Some are reimagined. All of them are watching.

The Sea Jinn – بودريا

From Bahraini folklore, بودريا is said to drown divers and pull swimmers to their deaths in unknown ways. ... In my world, he is one of the Darakthul Kingdom — the most feared kingdom among both Jinn and Humans. Nobody knows what he looks like. This folklore inspired the creation of Darakthul.

The Mother of a Donkey – أم حمار

A female jinn known to stand by roads at night asking someone to stop and ... give her a ride to her destination. The legend says that whoever picks her up is never seen again.
The only way to tell if she’s real is to look at her feet — they resemble a donkey’s.

Kong Koi – ผีกองกอย

A Thai folklore about a forest jinn that drains the blood ... of its victims. It has one leg, one hand, and a long tongue. Its victims are often those who get lost or wander off the road. It sneaks into camps at night when no one is awake.
Some have reported hearing faint whispers of “Koi... koi... koi...” in the dark.
